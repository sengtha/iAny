# Khmer Sign Language — community outreach

The `/sign` collector only produces a *good, correct* dataset if Deaf signers
lead it. Our starter word list is a scaffold; the authoritative sign for every
label must come from the Cambodian Deaf community. This is a ready-to-adapt
outreach kit for inviting a partner to co-lead the effort.

## Who to reach out to
Organisations working with the Deaf community and Khmer Sign Language (KSL) in
Cambodia — for example:

- **Deaf Development Programme (DDP)**, Phnom Penh — KSL training, interpreting,
  and the group historically closest to KSL documentation.
- **Krousar Thmey** — deaf & blind education; developed Khmer Braille and sign
  materials used in schools.
- **National Institute of Special Education (NISE)** and special-education
  teachers.
- **Cambodian Disabled People's Organisation (CDPO)** and local Deaf clubs /
  associations.
- University linguistics departments with sign-language researchers.

> Verify current contacts and leadership before sending — organisations change.
> Lead with listening: ask how *they* would want this built, and be ready to
> hand over direction of the vocabulary and the signs themselves.

## What we're asking for
1. **Validate the signs** — confirm the correct KSL sign for each label (and fix
   our starter list, which may be wrong or incomplete).
2. **Co-design the vocabulary** — which words/letters matter most to teach and
   recognise first.
3. **Involve Deaf signers as contributors** — they record the reference signs;
   they're credited (opt-in) in the open dataset.
4. **Guide the ethics** — consent, representation, and how the released model
   should and shouldn't be used.

## What we offer / commit to
- The tool records **hand landmarks only, never video** — no face or identity is
  stored.
- Dataset **and** model released **open (CC-BY-SA-4.0)**, free for schools,
  interpreters, and researchers — "with the community, for the community."
- Contributors and the partner org are **credited**.
- The partner **leads the linguistic direction**; we build and host the tech.
- We can add/relabel prompts, adjust the flow, or localise wording on request.

## Email / message template (English)

> **Subject:** Building a free, open Khmer Sign Language tool — seeking your guidance
>
> Dear [name / organisation],
>
> I'm building **iAny**, a free, offline Khmer AI project. We've released open
> Khmer speech-to-text, text-to-speech, and OCR, and we're now starting on
> **Khmer Sign Language recognition** — with the goal of releasing an open
> dataset and model that schools, interpreters, and the Deaf community can use
> freely.
>
> We've built a simple web page where a signer is shown a Khmer word and signs
> it to the camera. Importantly, it records **only the position of the hands
> (landmark points), never any video** — so no one's face or identity is ever
> stored.
>
> We know that KSL belongs to the Deaf community, and a tool like this is only
> right if Deaf signers lead it. I'd love your guidance on: which signs and
> words to start with, confirming the correct signs, and how to involve Deaf
> contributors fairly (with credit). Everything we collect and build will be
> released openly and free.
>
> Could we find 20 minutes to talk about whether and how to do this together?
>
> Thank you for your work,
> [Your name] · [contact] · [project link]

## After they say yes — a good first session
- Ask them to **review the starter list** (`src/assets/signPrompts.ts`) and mark
  what's wrong, missing, or low-priority.
- Record a **few reference signers** on `/sign` together, so you both see the
  flow and the data (and catch UX problems early).
- Agree on **credit wording** and the **license** (CC-BY-SA-4.0) in writing.
- Decide **coverage goals** — how many signers per label — and a schedule.
